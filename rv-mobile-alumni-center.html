Humboldt Spirit Hits the Road

By Iridian Casarez

The Forever Humboldt Mobile Alumni Center is impossible to miss. Big, bold, and decked out in Cal Poly Humboldt tributes, the vehicle has become a rolling celebration of connection, community, and Humboldt spirit.

The Mobile Alumni Center was designed to take the Humboldt spirit on the road, allowing alumni, families, and friends to gather and stay connected wherever they are. From Seattle to San Diego, the vehicle brings alumni, families, students, and supporters together through gatherings big and small—all with a little Lumberjack flair. Whether it's tailgates, parades, river days, mixers, or campus celebrations, the Mobile Alumni Center turns every stop into a chance to reconnect and celebrate what it means to be part of the Humboldt family.

Holiday Cheer

Spreading the Cal Poly Humboldt Holiday Cheer

When the holidays arrive, the Mobile Alumni Center trades sunshine for twinkle lights and jingle bells. During the annual Humboldt Truckers Parade in Eureka, the vehicle transforms into a rolling holiday spectacle. Imagine festive decorations, cheerful faces, and just enough Humboldt magic to feel right at home.

Race Day

Sand, Sneakers, and Running

The Clam Beach Run is already a local Humboldt tradition, but the Mobile Alumni Center makes the shoreline even more spirited. The vehicle brings an extra dose of energy to race day, giving alumni and supporters a place to gather, cheer each other on, and celebrate after crossing the finish line.

Alumni Mixers

Bringing Humboldt to Alumni

The Mobile Alumni Center regularly travels across the West Coast to host alumni mixers in cities such as Portland, Seattle, Modesto, Santa Cruz, and San Diego. Whenever alumni come together, the Mobile Alumni Center recreates a sense of Humboldt through shared stories, familiar faces, and abundant school pride.

Student Send-Off

Welcoming New Lumberjacks

During Student Send-Off events across California, the Mobile Alumni Center helps welcome future students and their families before they even arrive on campus. It's a chance to meet fellow Lumberjacks, hear from alumni, ask questions, and start building connections long before move-in day.

Tailgates

Tailgates and Team Spirit

Athletics and alumni gatherings are a natural fit, and the Mobile Alumni Center always arrives ready to celebrate both. From the Forever Humboldt Women's Rowing Tailgate to outings like Forever Humboldt at the San Francisco Giants game, the vehicle creates spaces where alumni can cheer, reconnect, and enjoy a little sports fun together.

Campus Traditions

The Heart of Campus Celebrations

Back on campus, the vehicle has become a familiar face at some of Cal Poly Humboldt's biggest traditions. During Lumberjack Weekend, the Mobile Alumni Center helps kick off a packed schedule of music, block parties, reunions, and festivities.

Bigfoot Daze

Bigfoot Country

Some events feel tailor-made for the Mobile Alumni Center. Every summer, the vehicle rolls into Willow Creek for Bigfoot Daze, joining the festivities in true Humboldt fashion. Alumni and community members gather at Veterans Park after the parade to enjoy music, games, food, and plenty of Forever Humboldt fun. It's part hometown celebration, part reunion, and entirely unforgettable.

River Days

River Days Done Right

What's a Humboldt summer without a river day? Forever Humboldt brought alumni and friends together at Camp Kimtu in Willow Creek for a classic Trinity River escape complete with sunshine, swimming, and laid-back summer vibes. It's the kind of gathering that feels uniquely Humboldt: equal parts adventure, relaxation, and community.

Where Will the Mobile Alumni Center Go Next?

That's part of the fun—you never quite know where the Mobile Alumni Center will appear next. One weekend, it's parked at an athletic event, the next it's rolling into a city for a mixer or lighting up a holiday parade. Wherever it travels, the Mobile Alumni Center carries a little piece of Humboldt along for the ride.

See where it's headed next at humboldt.edu/forever.
