The Mobile Alumni Center Hits the Road

This fall, a little piece of campus is coming to you. The Cal Poly Humboldt RV is trading its parking spot under the redwoods for the open road, carrying green-and-gold spirit down the California coast. Over four stops and more than 500 miles, it will reconnect Lumberjacks with each other, with the university, and with the places that made their college years unforgettable. Scroll along and ride shotgun.

Stop 1 · Humboldt

Where the Journey Begins

Every road trip needs a starting line, and ours sits right where it should: beneath the towering coast redwoods of Humboldt County. On a crisp September morning, the RV rolls out of Arcata with a full tank, a packed itinerary, and a send-off party worthy of the occasion.

Faculty, current students, and alumni who never strayed far from home gather to wish the Mobile Alumni Center well. There are handshakes, hugs, and more than a few stories about late nights in the library and foggy mornings on the quad. It is the perfect reminder of exactly what this journey is all about.

Stop 2 · Fort Bragg

Coastal Reunions

Two hours south, Highway 1 narrows and the Pacific opens up beside us. Fort Bragg greets the RV with rugged bluffs, salt air, and the steady rhythm of waves against the rocks. It is the kind of view that makes you slow down and remember why the North Coast gets under your skin.

Grads from across Mendocino County climb aboard to tour the revamped alumni center on wheels, sign the travel guestbook, and trade updates on careers, families, and the occasional fishing record. By the time the RV pulls away, a few new road-trip friends are already planning to meet up at the next stop.

Stop 3 · Santa Cruz

Sun, Surf & Stories

The fog lifts and the energy shifts as the RV rolls into Santa Cruz. The boardwalk hums, the sun is out, and the beach becomes an impromptu reunion hall. Recent graduates mix with alumni who walked the stage decades ago, proof that the Lumberjack bond does not fade with a diploma.

Over tacos and lemonade, conversations drift from favorite professors to first jobs to the wild ways life unfolds after college. A few brave souls even paddle out for a sunset surf. However they choose to celebrate, everyone leaves with sand in their shoes and a renewed sense of community.

Stop 4 · Monterey

A Bayside Send-Off

The final leg curves around Monterey Bay just as the sky turns gold. String lights flicker on, a local band tunes up, and the RV parks one last time against a backdrop of sailboats and sea otters. It is less a farewell than a victory lap.

Alumni raise a glass to the miles behind them and the friendships rekindled along the way. Before the night ends, the team is already mapping next year's route, because the Mobile Alumni Center has proven one thing for certain: home travels well, and the Lumberjack family is everywhere.

Catch the RV Near You

The Mobile Alumni Center is just getting started, and this first tour down the coast is only the beginning of the road. Keep an eye out for the green-and-teal rig rolling into a town near you, follow along on social media for route announcements, and when you spot it parked at a local park or pier, come say hello. There is always a seat saved for a fellow Lumberjack.
