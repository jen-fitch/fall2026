Jenkins Hall

Jenkins Hall after renovation.

Creative Spaces

Legacy of Craftsmanship

Originally opened in 1950 as the home of Humboldt’s Industrial Arts program, the building was named for Horace “Pop” Jenkins, a ceramicist and faculty member who championed hands-on learning. Following a $12 million renovation funded by the California State University’s Chancellor’s Office, the building re-opened in Fall 2025 as a modern home for the Sculpture and Ceramics programs, thoughtfully designed to support the next generation of artists.

Where Inspiration Begins

Forged, Fired, and Formed

The renovated facility now features studios, fabrication spaces, kiln rooms, and a 3D printing room where students can work with wood, metal, or clay. The complementary facilities allow students to move seamlessly from concept to finished work.

First Floor

Sculpture Lab

On the first floor is the Sculpture Lab. It includes areas for metalworking, woodworking, mold-making, mixed-media projects, and digital fabrication, so students can explore a wide range of artistic processes from concept to completion.

The Foundry

One of the building's most distinctive spaces is The Foundry. Here, students learn the art of metal casting—pouring molten metal into molds and transforming them into sculptures—an ancient process that remains central to contemporary sculpture.

Second Floor

Ceramics Studio

The Ceramics Studio supports every stage of the ceramic-making process. The studio includes a variety of facilities for students to explore both wheel throwing and handbuilding, developing their work from raw clay to finished pieces.

The studio has dedicated spaces for clay preparation, glazing, firing, and mold-making. Low- and high-fire facilities allow students to learn a variety of firing techniques, while the glaze room serves as a place for experimentation with color, texture, and surface design.

Together, these spaces foster an environment where artistry, creativity, and self-expression thrive.
